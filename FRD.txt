When developing a functional requirements document (FRD) for a clothing store website, it's beneficial to reference existing successful e-commerce platforms. Websites like Zara, H&M, GAP, Uniqlo, and ASOS exemplify effective implementations of key functionalities.

Key Functional Requirements:

User Management:

Registration and Login: Allow users to create accounts and log in using email or social media credentials.
Profile Management: Enable users to update personal information, view order history, and manage preferences.
Product Catalog:

Categorization: Organize products into categories (e.g., Men, Women, Kids) and subcategories (e.g., Tops, Bottoms).
Detailed Descriptions: Provide comprehensive product information, including multiple images, sizes, colors, materials, and care instructions.
Search and Filtering:

Search Functionality: Implement a search bar with auto-suggestions to help users find products quickly.
Filters: Allow users to filter products by size, color, price range, brand, and other attributes.
Shopping Cart and Checkout:

Cart Management: Enable users to add, remove, and adjust quantities of products in their cart.
Checkout Process: Provide a streamlined, secure checkout process with multiple payment options (e.g., credit/debit cards, digital wallets).
Order Management:

Order Tracking: Allow users to track the status of their orders in real-time.
Order History: Provide access to past orders and details.
Customer Support:

Live Chat: Offer real-time assistance through live chat support.
Contact Forms: Provide forms for inquiries, returns, and feedback.
Reviews and Ratings:

Customer Reviews: Allow customers to leave reviews and rate products.
Moderation: Implement moderation tools to manage user-generated content.
Security:

Data Protection: Ensure compliance with data protection regulations and implement SSL encryption for secure transactions.
User Privacy: Provide clear privacy policies and options for users to manage their data.
